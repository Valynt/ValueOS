 Executive Summary
 GenAI is rapidly reshaping the technology landscape, with over half of large organizations
 already deploying GenAI solutions and many more actively investing in pilots and
 production use cases. According to IDC’s latest research, the opportunity for GenAI
 in the market today is immense: Organizations are moving beyond experimentation to
 scale GenAI for real business impact, leveraging its capabilities to automate workflows,
 enhance decision-making, and unlock new sources of value across industries, while those
 who invest early and strategically will gain a significant competitive advantage as the
 technology matures and adoption accelerates globally.
 IDC conducted a Business Value study by interviewing organizations using
 Amazon Bedrock and Amazon SageMaker AI (AWS AI services) to develop, customize,
 and deploy GenAI models and applications. The research found that these
 AWS services accelerate GenAI model creation, streamline GenAI development
 workflows, and enhance the delivery of personalized, relevant AI- and ML-driven
 customer experiences. As a result, organizations reported notable improvements in
 GenAI-related accessibility, productivity, and operational efficiencies.
 IDC projects that interviewed customers will realize average annual benefits
 of $131,600 per GenAI use case ($5.63 million per organization) through using
 these AWS services and solutions by:
 • Accelerating GenAI model delivery and application development with standardized
 workflows and interfaces, ease of customization, and robust security and governance
 • Improving personalization and customer experience through delivery of relevant
 and high-performing GenAI applications and use cases, which drives higher revenue
 by increasing customer engagement and meeting customer needs
 • Increasing the productivity and effectiveness of teams responsible for GenAI
 and ML-driven applications and use cases, including model development,
 model customization, and infrastructure management, by minimizing manual effort
 and complexity
 • Ensuring stronger governance and security through built-in controls and standardized
 interfaces that support compliance, reduce privacy risk, and improve oversight of
 GenAI models and use cases

 Situation Overview
 GenAI is rapidly transitioning from experimental pilots to broad adoption, with nearly half
 of organizations already deploying GenAI-enhanced applications in production and
 the majority expecting significant increases in GenAI investment over the next year
 (IDC’s Future Enterprise Resiliency and Spending Survey, Wave 10, November 2024).
 Over the next few years, GenAI will drive transformative business value by automating
 tasks, enhancing decision-making, and enabling new use cases across industries, with early
 adopters gaining a competitive advantage as the technology matures and becomes central
 to digital innovation strategies.
 Amazon Bedrock
 and Amazon SageMaker AI
 Amazon Bedrock
 Amazon Bedrock is AWS’s platform for building generative AI applications and agents at
 production scale. It provides unified access to a wide variety of foundation models from
 leading providers such as Anthropic, DeepSeek, Mistral AI, Meta, OpenAI, and Amazon’s own
 Nova family of models, all accessible via a single API. Amazon Bedrock enables enterprises to
 quickly experiment, customize, and deploy GenAI solutions with enterprise-grade security,
 privacy, and governance.
 Key features of Amazon Bedrock:
 • Model choice and customization:
 Amazon Bedrock offers access to hundreds of top foundation models, including Amazon
 Nova models (text, image, document, video, and interactive speech), and supports custom
 model import, fine-tuning, and retrieval-augmented generation workflows. This flexibility
 allows organizations to select the optimal model for their use case, balancing accuracy,
 speed, and cost.
 • Agentic AI capabilities:
 The latest innovation is Amazon Bedrock AgentCore, an agentic platform for building,
 deploying, and operating effective agents securely at scale. AgentCore includes runtime,
 short- and long-term memory, a gateway for tool discovery and integration, identity
 management, observability, a code interpreter, browser automation, quality evaluations
 and policy controls. These services support complex, long-running agent workflows and
 multistep tasks with enterprise-grade reliability and security.

 Amazon SageMaker
 Amazon SageMaker AI is AWS’s flagship platform for ML and AI model development,
 supporting the entire AI/ML lifecycle from data preparation to model development,
 deployment, monitoring, and governance.
 Key features of Amazon SageMaker:
 • Integrated development environment:
 SageMaker Unified Studio integrates code-first and visual development tools,
 low-code/no-code GenAI app builders (SageMaker Canvas), natural language–based
 development (Amazon Q), and Git-based collaboration. It provides seamless access to
 data across Amazon S3, SageMaker Lakehouse, Redshift, and federated sources.
 • Comprehensive ML and AI model lifecycle:
 SageMaker AI supports traditional ML models, GenAI foundation model development
 (pretraining, fine-tuning, and distillation), AI model customization, and agentic AI
 development. It offers tools for data labeling, cleansing, enrichment, bias detection
 (Clarify), explainability, model registry, and monitoring (Model Monitor).
 • Scalability and performance:
 Features such as SageMaker AI HyperPod enable distributed training across thousands
 of accelerators (including the latest EC2 P6e-GB200 UltraServers with NVIDIA
 Blackwell GPUs), supporting large-scale model development. Real-time inferencing
 and streaming capabilities are tightly integrated for low-latency, high-throughput
 predictions.

 Appendix A:
 IDC Business Value Methodology
 IDC utilized its standard Business Value methodology for this white paper.
 This methodology is based on gathering data from organizations that are currently using
 Amazon Bedrock and Amazon SageMaker AI to develop and run GenAI applications
 and use cases as the foundation for the model. Based on interviews with these study
 participants, IDC has calculated the benefits and costs to these organizations related to
 their use of Amazon Bedrock and Amazon SageMaker AI. IDC conducted its quantitative
 analysis for this study by gathering benefit information during the interviews using a
 before-and-after assessment of the impact of using these AWS GenAI solutions. In
 this study, the benefits included efficiencies and productivity gains for GenAI model
 and development teams, GenAI-driven net revenue gains, productivity increases,
 infrastructure and tool cost savings, and other IT team efficiencies.
 IDC applied its standard Business Value methodology assumptions for financial
 calculations, which did not derive from the primary research:
 Salary assumptions:
 • IT staff: $100,000/year (fully loaded)
 • Non-IT staff: $70,000/year (fully loaded)
 • Work hours: 1,880/year (47 weeks × 40 hours)
 Discounting of certain benefits:
 • IDC applied a 15% realization factor to revenue gains, productivity gains, and
 operational cost savings resulting from GenAI enablement to avoid overestimating
 benefits directly related to using Amazon Bedrock and Amazon SageMaker AI and
 account for other influencing factors.
