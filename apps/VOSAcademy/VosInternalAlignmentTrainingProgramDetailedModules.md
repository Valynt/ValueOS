# VOS Internal Alignment Training Program: Detailed Modules

This document expands the 10-Pillar Internal Alignment Training Program into comprehensive, modular training modules. Each pillar is designed as a self-contained unit (approximately 5-7 pages when formatted for slides or webpages, equating to 1,500-2,500 words per pillar), suitable for 2-4 hour sessions. Modules emphasize practical application through hands-on exercises, assessments, and outputs, while aligning with the VOS Maturity Model (Levels 0-5) and role-specific tracks (Sales, Customer Success [CS], Marketing, Product, Executive Leadership, Value Engineering [VE]). Content draws from the VOS framework, integrating elements like KPI definition sheets (Pillars 1-2), discovery framework questions (Pillar 3), Value Commit templates (Pillar 4), and playbook content (Pillar 8). Educational elements include visuals (e.g., diagrams, tables), role-plays, and quizzes for engagement. For higher maturity (Levels 3-5), AI-augmented suggestions (e.g., prompts for GenAI tools) enhance scalability. Total program: ~50,000 words, with capstone certification via simulation.

## Pillar 1: Unified Value Language

### Objectives
By the end of this module, participants will:
- Define and apply a consistent enterprise-wide value language focused on measurable outcomes rather than features.
- Normalize all value discussions to the Revenue/Cost/Risk framework, reducing miscommunication across functions.
- Understand and use standardized KPI taxonomy to align teams on economic impact.
- Achieve foundational alignment (Maturity Level 1: Value Awareness), enabling role-specific adaptations like Sales shifting from feature pitches to outcome framing.

This pillar addresses Level 0 chaos (inconsistent "value dialects") by introducing basic language, progressing to Level 2 shared foundations where KPIs emerge consistently.

### In-Depth Topics with Explanations
**Topic 1: What is "Value"? (Enterprise Definition)**  
Value in VOS is not subjective benefits but quantifiable economic impact tied to business outcomes. At Level 0, teams often confuse value with features (e.g., "Our tool has AI analytics" vs. "This reduces manual reporting by 30%, saving $X in labor costs"). Explanation: Value originates from customer problems solved through capabilities, measured via KPIs, and normalized to Revenue (e.g., increased sales efficiency), Cost (e.g., operational savings), or Risk (e.g., compliance reduction). This ensures cross-functional consistency—Sales uses it for pitches, CS for proof, Product for roadmaps. Draw from KPI sheets: Value must link to formulas like Lead Conversion Rate (% leads to pipeline, data source: CRM), avoiding vague terms.

**Topic 2: Revenue/Cost/Risk Framework**  
This core framework unifies discussions: Revenue focuses on uplift (e.g., faster onboarding cycles boosting MRR); Cost on savings (e.g., reduced manual hours); Risk on mitigation (e.g., error rates lowered). Explanation: At Level 1, teams begin applying this reactively (e.g., late-stage ROI mentions); by Level 2, it's proactive (e.g., Marketing campaigns tied to revenue KPIs). Role adaptations: Sales maps pains to revenue gaps; CS tracks cost variances in QBRs. Use KPI sheet examples: Onboarding Cycle Time (formula: Go-Live Date - Kickoff Date; benchmark: 20-35 days; impact: Revenue via faster time-to-value; leading indicators: Training completion rate).

**Topic 3: KPI Taxonomy & Standard Definitions**  
KPIs are standardized metrics in the Value Fabric ontology. Explanation: Taxonomy categorizes by type (leading/lagging, e.g., MQL volume as leading for Lead Conversion Rate) and impact (financial mapping: e.g., Manual Hours Reduced → Cost Savings). At Level 0, KPIs vary by region/team; Level 1 introduces definitions (e.g., from sheets: Definition, Formula, Data Source, Benchmarks). Role-specific: Executives tie to OKRs; VE governs taxonomy. Pitfalls: Overloading with non-financial metrics—focus on traceable outcomes.

**Visual Aid: Framework Diagram**  
A triangular diagram: Revenue (top), Cost (left), Risk (right), with KPI examples branching out (e.g., Revenue → Lead Conversion Rate table below).

| KPI Example | Definition | Formula | Data Source | Benchmark | Impact |
|-------------|------------|---------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Lead Conversion Rate | % of leads converting to pipeline | (Qualified Leads / Total Leads) × 100 | CRM (e.g., Salesforce) | 15-25% | Revenue Uplift |
| Manual Hours Reduced | Hours saved from automation | Baseline Hours - Post-Implementation Hours | Time-tracking tools | 20-40% reduction | Cost Savings |

### Hands-On Exercises
- **Group Mapping Activity (45 mins):** In role-mixed teams (e.g., Sales + CS), rephrase 5 feature statements into Revenue/Cost/Risk terms using KPI sheet examples (e.g., "AI dashboard" → "Reduces reporting time by 25%, saving $50K annually in costs"). Output: Shared whiteboard or digital canvas.
- **KPI Application Role-Play (30 mins):** Pairs simulate a cross-functional meeting (e.g., Sales pitches to Product using taxonomy); adapt for roles (Marketing: Create outcome-based tagline).

### Knowledge Checks
- **Interactive Quiz (20 mins, 15 questions):** Multiple-choice (e.g., "Which framework normalizes value? A) Feature/Benefit B) Revenue/Cost/Risk") and scenario-based (e.g., "Map this KPI to impact: Onboarding Cycle Time"). Discussion follow-up: 10-min group debate on a real-world inconsistency from Level 0 symptoms.
- **Pass Threshold:** 80%; retake with feedback for non-passers.

### Specific Outputs
- **Internal Glossary:** Digital document (Google Doc/PDF) with 50+ terms (e.g., "Value Tree: Hierarchical map from capabilities to outcomes"; "KPI: Standardized metric per sheet structure"). Co-edited in session; version-controlled for updates.
- **Personal Value Lexicon Card:** One-page handout for daily use, customized by role (e.g., Sales: Top 10 sales-aligned KPIs).

### Maturity Model Alignments for Role-Specific Adaptations
- **Level 0-1 (Awareness):** All roles focus on basics—Sales: Identify pains via framework; CS: Track simple outcomes manually.
- **Level 2 (Alignment):** Sales/CS use taxonomy in handoffs; Marketing: KPI-driven narratives; Product: Early KPI definitions in roadmaps; Executives: Sponsor lexicon; VE: Publish sheets.
- **Level 3+ (Integration):** Roles adapt for lifecycle (e.g., Product instruments KPIs); higher levels tie to AI (e.g., Level 4 Sales: AI-suggested KPI mappings).

### AI-Augmented Elements (for Levels 3-5)

## Pillar 2: Value Data Model Mastery

### Objectives
Participants will:
- Master the structure and purpose of the VOS Value Data Model (Value Fabric), enabling accurate value quantification.
- Build and interpret Value Trees to connect capabilities to financial outcomes.
- Apply data collection standards to feed the ROI Engine, reducing errors in modeling.
- Align to Maturity Level 2 (Value Alignment), with role adaptations like CS using trees for realization plans.

This pillar bridges Level 1 awareness (emerging KPIs) to Level 2 foundations (initial Value Trees), addressing gaps like no shared architecture.

### In-Depth Topics with Explanations
**Topic 1: Value Tree Hierarchy**  
The Value Tree is a hierarchical model in the Value Fabric, mapping from product capabilities (e.g., automation tools) to use cases (e.g., streamlined reporting), value drivers (e.g., efficiency gains), KPIs (e.g., hours reduced), and financial outcomes (e.g., $X cost savings). Explanation: At Level 1, trees are ad-hoc; Level 2 standardizes for core solutions (e.g., using KPI sheets: Manual Hours Reduced as driver, formula: Baseline - Post-Hours). Role-specific: Product builds trees for roadmaps; VE owns hierarchy to ensure traceability.

**Topic 2: Inputs to the ROI Engine**  
The ROI Engine processes tree data into models. Explanation: Inputs include standardized collection (e.g., from sheets: Data Sources like CRM for Lead Conversion Rate; Benchmarks: 15-25%). Conservative inputs prevent overpromising—e.g., use lagging indicators (e.g., actual revenue) over leading (e.g., leads). At Level 2, templates reuse inputs; Level 3 automates via CRM. Role adaptations: Sales inputs discovery data; CS validates post-sale.

**Topic 3: Data Collection Standards**  
Standards ensure quality (e.g., validation rules from sheets: Leading Indicators like MQL for early signals; Lagging like pipeline value for confirmation). Explanation: Address gaps (e.g., missing telemetry) with workflows. Use Cases: Financial Mapping (e.g., Onboarding Cycle Time → Revenue via faster MRR). Pitfalls: Inconsistent sources leading to Level 0 reactivity.

**Visual Aid: Value Tree Diagram**  
Hierarchical flowchart: Capabilities (root) → Use Cases → Drivers → KPIs (with sheet details) → Outcomes. Example table for inputs:

| Input Type | Example from Sheet | Standard | Role Use |
|------------|--------------------|----------|----------|
| KPI | Onboarding Cycle Time | Formula: (Go-Live - Kickoff); Benchmark: 20-35 days | CS: Track in plans |
| Benchmark | Lead Conversion Rate | 15-25%; Data: CRM | Sales: Hypothesis building |

### Hands-On Exercises
- **Building a Value Tree (60 mins):** Individuals/groups use digital tool (e.g., Lucidchart) to construct a tree for a core product (e.g., map "AI analytics capability" to "Manual Hours Reduced" KPI → $50K cost savings). Incorporate sheet formulas; peer review for hierarchy accuracy. Role-adapt: Marketing adds narrative branches.
- **Data Input Simulation (30 mins):** Role-play collecting inputs (e.g., Sales "gathers" from mock discovery), validating against standards.

### Knowledge Checks
- **Quiz/Discussion (25 mins, 12 questions):** Drag-and-drop (match tree levels) and open-ended (e.g., "Explain ROI Engine input for this KPI sheet example"). Follow with 15-min discussion: "How does this apply to your role at Level 2?"
- **Pass Threshold:** 75%; includes self-reflection on data gaps.

### Specific Outputs
- **Value Tree Template:** Editable workbook (Excel/Google Sheets) with pre-populated sheet examples; participants export personal version.
- **Data Standards Checklist:** One-page tool for collection (e.g., "Verify source? Conservative benchmark?"), tied to ROI inputs.

### Maturity Model Alignments for Role-Specific Adaptations
- **Level 1-2:** Basics—Sales: Preliminary trees in deals; CS: Manual inputs for plans.
- **Level 3 (Integration):** Full lifecycle trees (e.g., Product: Telemetry-linked); VE: Expand coverage.
- **Level 4+:** Predictive trees (e.g., Marketing: AI-generated inputs).
- **Adaptation Table:**

| Role | Level 2 Adaptation | Level 4 Adaptation |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Sales | Template trees for ROI | AI-assisted tree building |
| CS | Input for realization dashboards | Automated variance inputs |
| Marketing | Tree-mapped content | Predictive driver suggestions |
| Product | KPI-tied roadmaps | Telemetry-integrated trees |
| Executives | High-level oversight | Fabric-based forecasting |
| VE | Core tree creation | Sensitivity analysis inputs |

### AI-Augmented Elements (for Levels 3-5)
- **Prompt Suggestion:** Level 3+: "Based on this KPI sheet [insert Onboarding Cycle Time details], generate a Value Tree hierarchy for a SaaS onboarding product, linking to cost savings. Ensure conservative benchmarks." Use for exercise drafts, with validation step.

## Pillar 3: Discovery Excellence

### Objectives
Participants will:
- Conduct structured discovery to quantify pains and structure problems using unified methods.
- Apply persona frameworks and pain quantification to build value hypotheses early.
- Generate actionable outputs for lifecycle transitions, aligning to Level 2 (Value Alignment) for consistent qualification.
- Role-adapt: Sales for opportunity qualification; CS for renewal discovery.

This pillar targets Level 1 gaps (varied discovery) by standardizing questions, progressing to Level 3 integrated flows.

### In-Depth Topics with Explanations
**Topic 1: Persona Frameworks**  
Personas define roles (e.g., CFO: Cost-focused; Ops Lead: Efficiency pains). Explanation: Use framework questions: "What KPIs does your role own? How are those measured today?" At Level 1, basic identification; Level 2 tailors to Value Trees. Role: Marketing creates persona stories; VE maps to Fabric.

**Topic 2: Pain Quantification**  
Quantify barriers (e.g., "What prevents KPI progress? What would improvement look like numerically?"). Explanation: Tie to sheets (e.g., lagging KPIs like conversion rates). Level 2: Score pains (high-impact on Revenue); avoid qualitative vagueness from Level 0.

**Topic 3: Problem Structuring**  
Map pains to context (e.g., "What systems touch workflows? What data is missing?"). Explanation: Structure into hypotheses (e.g., "Low conversion due to manual processes → $X revenue gap"). Integrate with lifecycle: Outputs feed Pillar 4 cases. Role: Product identifies telemetry needs.

**Visual Aid: Discovery Flowchart**  
Linear diagram: Persona ID → Pain Questions → Quantification → Structuring → Hypothesis. Table of sample questions:

| Category | Framework Question | Example Response | Link to KPI Sheet |
|----------|--------------------|------------------|-------------------|

| Pains & Gaps | What prevents progress? | Manual data entry delays | Manual Hours Reduced; cost impact |
| Operational Context | What data is missing? | Adoption metrics | Onboarding Cycle Time; leading indicators |

### Hands-On Exercises
- **Discovery Role-Play (50 mins):** Trios simulate sessions (one "customer," one discoverer, one observer) using framework questions (e.g., quantify pain for a persona). Record and debrief; adapt for roles (CS: Renewal focus).
- **Pain Mapping Workshop (40 mins):** Groups structure 3 pains into a hypothesis tree, incorporating sheet benchmarks.

### Knowledge Checks
- **Quiz/Discussion (20 mins, 14 questions):** Scenario matching (e.g., "Best question for pain: A) KPI ownership B) System integration"). 10-min discussion: Apply to real deal/renewal.
- **Pass Threshold:** 80%.

### Specific Outputs
- **Pain Quant Scorecard:** Excel tool (from framework): Score pains (1-10 on impact/feasibility), with columns for questions, responses, KPI links.
- **Discovery Script Template:** Customizable guide with 20+ questions, role-adapted versions.

### Maturity Model Alignments for Role-Specific Adaptations
- **Level 1-2:** Basic frameworks—Sales: Early pain ID; CS: Manual tracking.
- **Level 3:** Lifecycle integration (e.g., Product: Data gap closure).
- **Level 4+:** AI-assisted (e.g., Executives: Predictive persona insights).
- **Adaptation Table:**

| Role | Level 2 Adaptation | Level 3 Adaptation |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Sales | Structured qualification | Hypothesis to Commit transition |
